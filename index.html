Background Information

What is an OTU?

An OTU is an Operational Taxonomic Unit. It is used to classify bacteria based on sequence similarity of the 16S marker gene. An OTU consists of a group of bacteria whose 16S marker gene shows a sequence similarity of 97% and higher.

In the 'Top Ten Bacteria Cultures Found' chart, when you hover over the OTU ID bars, you will see the bacteria species associated with the OTU ID.
